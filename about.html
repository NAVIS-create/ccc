Our History

Decades of dominance in Sri Lankan cricket.

The Legacy

The Colombo Cricket Club (CCC) is a first-class cricket club in Sri Lanka. It is the oldest in the country, having been formed in 1863, and is headquartered at 31 Maitland Crescent, Colombo 7, close to the headquarters of Sri Lanka Cricket.

1832 - The Genesis

The idea of a gentlemen's team for colonists in Ceylon was first mooted among the patrons of the Colombo Club. The beginnings of the club proper trace back to a notice in the Colombo Journal of 5 September 1832, calling for gentlemen to form a Cricket Club, which was officially established later that year.

November 1832 - First Recorded Match

Located initially in Slave Island, the club played the first officially recorded game of cricket in the country against a team fielded by the 97th Regiment of the British Army. The club soon became the de facto governing body for cricket in Ceylon.

1863 - The Gymkhana Expansion

The club expanded to become the Colombo Gymkhana Club, an umbrella organisation administering various sports clubs including the CCC. The club was formally named with its current name and relocated to Galle Face Green during this period.

1894 & Beyond - A Lasting Legacy

In 1894, the club moved to its present address at Maitland Crescent. Opening its doors beyond European-only membership in 1962, the CCC celebrated its monumental 150th anniversary in 2013, standing today as a pillar of Sri Lankan cricket.

Our Vision

To be the driving force behind Sri Lanka's international cricket resurgence, fostering an environment where elite talent is nurtured, and sporting excellence is celebrated on a global stage.

Our Mission

To provide cutting-edge facilities, unparalleled coaching, and competitive pathways for athletes while upholding the rich traditions and integrity of the gentleman's game.

The premier cricket club in Sri Lanka. Forging international champions since 1892.
